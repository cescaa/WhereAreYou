Chapter 2

Old News

Robin was surprised at how public Jessica Bratford kept her profile.
She figured she would’ve wanted her personal life hidden from students,
even if most of her posts were just cat videos,
innocent memes only teachers would understand,
and stream of consciousness-esque status updates.
Lucky me, Robin thought. Someone so open on Facebook, especially a teacher, would probably have less reservations revealing student information to a complete stranger. Robin sent her a message and attached the photo of her and Sam. What she was doing could be stalking, but at least Jessica would know it was for good intentions.

Her message back was polite, but sprinkled with enough kindness and emojis not to feel cold and distant.

What high school extracurricular was Sam involved in?

Nope, try again!

Another dead end.

Finding Sam was starting to feel impossible. Robin stared at the teacher’s message and took a deep breath. Maybe she could find something about the contest, but where would she begin?
She opened another tab and typed Martin Grove High School student photography winner into Google.

94.4 million results in 0.74 seconds. That’s a start.

Robin scanned through them, clicking the ones that looked the most promising. On her 30th or so click, she found it. During Sam's senior year, she won a country-wide photography contest and the winning photo was featured in her local gallery.

And there it was—her last name—printed on the award she clutched close to her chest.

What is Sam's last name?

Nope, try again!
